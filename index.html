Yellow Fever Medical Information

Comprehensive medical information about yellow fever transmission, clinical manifestations, and prevention strategies for healthcare professionals and patients.

Viral Transmission & Cellular Pathology

Yellow fever virus (YFV) enters the human host through the bite of an infected Aedes aegypti mosquito vector. The virus is transmitted when the arthropod injects virion-containing saliva into the dermal and subcutaneous tissues during blood feeding.

Important Medical Information

Yellow fever is a serious viral infection that can be fatal. Early recognition and supportive care are critical for patient outcomes.

Figure 1: Aedes aegypti mosquito - Primary vector for yellow fever transmission

Viral Transmission Mechanism

1. Viral Inoculation

Infected Aedes aegypti mosquito injects yellow fever virus particles into human skin during blood feeding.

2. Systemic Circulation

Virus enters bloodstream and circulates throughout the body, targeting specific organ systems.

3. Hepatic Tropism

Virus shows preference for liver cells (hepatocytes), leading to primary hepatic infection and damage.

Clinical Manifestations & Systemic Effects

Yellow fever presents with biphasic clinical manifestations affecting multiple organ systems. The disease progression involves acute febrile phase followed by potential toxic phase with multi-organ dysfunction and hemorrhagic complications.

Clinical Alert

Only 15-20% of patients progress to toxic phase. Early recognition is critical for patient management.
